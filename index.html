OVERVIEW

WHAT IS WASTE MANAGEMENT ?

Waste management is the systematic handling of waste materials from their generation to disposal in a safe, efficient, and environmentally responsible manner. It involves collection, transportation, processing, recycling, and disposal of waste to minimize its impact on human health and the environment. Strategies include waste reduction, reuse, recycling, and energy recovery. Proper waste management aims to mitigate pollution, conserve resources, and promote sustainable practices. It encompasses various types of waste, including solid, liquid, and hazardous materials, addressing the complex challenges of urbanization, industrialization, and consumption patterns in modern societies.

We belive that if we harm our environment it will defenitely harm us.


OUR MISSION

The mission of a waste management system is to minimize environmental impact and protect human health by efficiently collecting, transporting, processing, and disposing of waste. It aims to promote sustainable practices, resource conservation, and pollution prevention through innovative solutions and community engagement.

THE VISION

"To create a world where waste is minimized, resources are conserved, and the environment is protected for future generations. We envision a circular economy where waste is seen as a valuable resource, and innovative technologies and sustainable practices drive towards zero waste and a cleaner, healthier planet."

ENVIRONMENT DAY

We globally observe the environment day on June 5th each year and often focuses on themes related to environmental conservation, including waste management. Additionally, some regions or organizations may designate specific days or weeks to raise awareness about waste management and its importance
